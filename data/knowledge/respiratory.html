Respiratory System

Overview

The respiratory system is responsible for gas exchange between the body and the external environment, bringing oxygen into the body and removing carbon dioxide.

Key Components

  • Nose and nasal cavity: Filters, warms, and humidifies air.
  • Pharynx (throat): Passageway for both air and food.
  • Larynx (voice box): Contains vocal cords and helps protect the airway.
  • Trachea (windpipe): Tube that connects the larynx to the bronchi.
  • Bronchi and bronchioles: Branches of airways that lead to the alveoli.
  • Lungs: Main organs of respiration containing alveoli where gas exchange occurs.
  • Diaphragm: Main muscle of respiration that contracts to create negative pressure in the thoracic cavity.

Lung Divisions

Right Lung: Divided into three lobes - superior, middle, and inferior.

Left Lung: Divided into two lobes - superior and inferior (has a cardiac notch to accommodate the heart).

Respiration Process

  1. Ventilation: Movement of air into and out of the lungs.
  2. External respiration: Gas exchange between alveoli and pulmonary capillaries.
  3. Transport: Movement of gases through the bloodstream.
  4. Internal respiration: Gas exchange between systemic capillaries and tissue cells.

Clinical Significance

Common respiratory conditions include:

  • Asthma: Chronic inflammatory disease causing airway narrowing and obstruction.
  • COPD: Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, including emphysema and chronic bronchitis.
  • Pneumonia: Infection causing inflammation of the alveoli.
  • Lung cancer: Uncontrolled growth of abnormal cells in the lung.
